The volume of a sphere is given by the formula \( V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \). If the radius \( r \) of a sphere is doubled, what happens to its volume?
- The volume becomes 8 times larger.
- The volume becomes 4 times larger.
- The volume remains.
- The volume becomes 2 times larger.
Explanation: When the radius is doubled, the new volume \( V' \) is calculated as \( V' = \frac{4}{3}\pi (2r)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi 8r^3 = 8V \). Therefore, the volume becomes 8 times larger.
\[ V' = \frac{4}{3}\pi (2r)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi 8V = \frac{4}{3}\pi 8(2r)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi 8V = 8V \]
Therefore, the volume increases by a factor of 8.
